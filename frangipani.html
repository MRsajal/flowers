Also known as plumeria, these flowers boast mesmerizing petals, often found in hues ranging from pure whites to vibrant pinks, yellows, and reds, creating a captivating display against glossy, dark green leaves. Their heady scent, sweet and floral, perfumes the air, making them a beloved addition to gardens in warm climates. Beyond their aesthetic charm, frangipani holds deep cultural and symbolic significance. In various cultures, they represent different ideas, from beauty, grace, and positivity to spirituality and the cycle of life. Often used in leis or floral arrangements, these blooms are celebrated for their ability to infuse spaces with tranquility and their reminder of the tropical allure of faraway lands.
